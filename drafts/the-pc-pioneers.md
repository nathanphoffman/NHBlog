# The 9 True Pioneers of the PC
We have all heard the story that Jobs and Gates are responsible for the personal computer revolution. Maybe you have even heard of Gate's partner Paul Allen or Job's partner Steve Wozniak, who arguably 
made larger contributions (especially the former). There is no denying that without these guys, the personal computer would have been different -- but I surmise it still would exist. At the time they 
were hacking hardware and installing software, there were already dozens (if not hundreds) of hobbiests doing the same, they were not in anyway unique, they were just better business people and faster -- 
able to get the first to market.

To me, being lucky, opportunistic, and good at business does not make you a pioneer. Being a pioneer means you truly come up with a revolutionary idea, you lay a foundation upon which everything else 
is built. Even the iPhone, which arguably may be jobs lasting legacy, was not something he pioneered, he actually argued against it, saying:

> Why the f*** would we want to do that? That is the dumbest idea I've ever heard.

Another problem is that many technologies we have came from corporations, or corporate funded labs like Bell Labs where ideas and innovators are hard to truly identify since it is all part of a shared 
funded team-framework. 


## Charles Babbage, *1820-1840*
*The first to conceive of a general purpose machine with memory, a processor, and conditionals*

## Ada Lovelace, *1843*
*The first to envision that a general purpose machine does not need to be limited to mathematics* 

## George Boole, *1854*
*Created an entire branch of mathematics that would make the transistor and modern computers possible*

## Alan Turing, *1936-1950*
*The first to mathematically prove and demonstrate that a general purpose computer is possible*

## Claude Shannon, *1937-1948*
*Showed how bits and binary could be used to encode information -- the final pathway to electronic computers*

## Grace Hopper, *1952-1959*
*The first compiler and the first to champion readable code -- forsight that computers would extend to everyone*

## Gary Kildall, **
*The first to prove a PC was actually possibly by developing the 1st viable OS for a microprocessor*

## Ed Roberts
*The first to see PC hardware as being something more useful than a calculator to a consumer*

## Richard Stallman
*The first to champion software controlled by users (GPl/Open Source) -- and a pioneer in early software*

## Linus Torvalds
*The first to develop a modern open source kernel --now the most used OS - ever (Android/Servers/Ubuntu)*
