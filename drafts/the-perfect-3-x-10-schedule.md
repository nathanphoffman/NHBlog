# The Perfect 3 x 10 Schedule

It is Monday, your alarm wakes you up as you look at the time: 6:30am. You think “I just need 5 more minutes” hit snooze and it goes off again. You aren’t feeling it; you are exhausted; yesterday you were moving your friend into their new home and were out till 12am.

But rather than force yourself to get up, you shrug and go back to sleep, disabling your alarm. The sunlight pours into the room, it is now 11:15am! But no worries, today you will go on a hike, go to the gym, or do anything else that isn’t working.

Why? Because you only have to work 3 days a week, and can choose the days you work since you have 5 to choose from.

## **The 5 day work week**

The biggest issue with the 5-day work week is in the name. It requires that workers work 5-consecutive days in a row. If I was forced to do anything 5-days in a row I would grow to view it as a chore. Go to the gym 5 days in a row, eat the same great food 5-days in a row, see the same friend 5-days in a row, you name it! But what is even worse is that workers usually want the weekend off (so they are off the same days as their friends / family) than this leaves no flexibility in days worked. M-F must then be worked by definition. There are no other 5-days to work.

## The 4 day work week

The 4-day work week lessens consecutive days to a maximum of 4 but has the added benefit of allowing a single day off in 5 different configurations:
- _TWRF
- M_WRF
- MT_WRF
- MTW_F
- MTWR_

## The 3 day work week

The 3-day week, on the other-hand. Allows workers to keep the weekend but also has an additional benefit in that it can be worked in 10 different configurations! One of them even allows workers to never work more than one day in a row: Mon, Wednesday, Friday.
- MTW__
- _TWR_
- __WRF
- M_W_F
- MT_R_
- MT__F
- M_WR_
- M__RF
- _T_RF
- _TW_F

## What about Less than 3?

The problem with working less than 3 days a week comes down to three basic problems:
- **Alignment:** It is possible on a M-F schedule (choosing only two days or less) to never align even one day with someone else. This means no ability to connect, meet or get unblocked on an item. Three days on the other-hand guarantees at least one overlap day.
- **Burnout is already minimal on 3:** Because a three day work week could be worked M,W,F where not a single day is worked in consecutive order, it wouldn’t be necessary to work less than that for the purposes of burnout.
- **There is only so much productivity to boost:** Many studies on the 4-day work week have shown that despite the lack of hours, a similar amount of work gets done. This would suggest a 25% increase in productivity on a 4 day work week, significant but not at all surprising. A three day work week would require a 67% increase in productivity to be competitive with a 5-day week, still possible but becoming more unlikely, but a 2 day work week would require a 250% increase in productivity. This seems unlikely. This would mean for someone to make a 2-day week worth it, they would have to have worked 2.5 times less than their maximal performance on a 5-day workweek, and an employee squandering that much time is unlikely to perform at 100% even on a 2-day workweek.

## **Breakdown**

Let us assume a worker has 90 minutes of meetings every day. This would mean **32.5, 26, 19.5** hours worked for a 5-day, 4-day, and 3-day work week.

However, since workers who work only 3 days probably won’t mind working 10 rather than 8 hours per day, especially since it is only on 3 days, let us make the 3-day work week at +2 hours / day (+6 total) and leave the other work weeks as 8-hour days.

 —> **32.5, 26.0, 25.5**

Now let us assume our worker gets 4 weeks vacation on the 5 & 4 day weeks and remove 2 weeks for the 3-day week (the reasoning here is with a flexible schedule they can already take 4 days off every week). This adds 2 weeks or +3.8% which is

— > **32.5, 26.0, 26.5**

Now we also know from many studies that workers on a 4-day work week perform the same amount of work to those on a 5-day schedule. This would suggest they work more efficiently. As discussed before, this would be an efficiency gain of 25%. But let us assume there is some bias to this number and go with a more conservative +20% increase in productivity for both the 4 and 3 day work weeks:

— > **32.5, 31.2, 31.8**

One might think that a 3-day work week would boost productivity even more, but since we are asking workers to work 10 and not 8 hours it is safe to assume there would likely not be that much of a measurable difference in productivity due to the added burnout of the slightly longer day and the decreasing gains in performance efficiency.

Lastly we need to discuss turnover, while we don’t have much turnover data on a 4-day and 3-day work week we do for the 5 day. It takes 6–9 months of salary to replace an employee ([https://www.peoplekeep.com/blog/employee-retention-the-real-cost-of-losing-an-employee](https://www.peoplekeep.com/blog/employee-retention-the-real-cost-of-losing-an-employee)) The average turn-over rate is 1–2 years for a developer according to: [https://www.zippia.com/software-developer-jobs/demographics/](https://www.zippia.com/software-developer-jobs/demographics/). This means that we can assume a nearly 25% waste due to costs associated to turn-overs. Even if we assume this is wildly exaggerated and this is only 15%, and then assume we would only reduce turnover of a 4-day and 3-day work-week by 1/3 (33%), we get a 5% adjustment, which is:

— > **32.5, 32.8, 33.4**

These numbers certainly make a valid case from a financial stand point for all three schedules, but as an executive which would you rather work in? —
- 5-day x 8 hours: An environment where people are burnt out and miserable and only getting work done because people are on the hook for so many hours.
- 4-day x 8 hours: A lively environment where workers are happier and even getting about 20 minutes more done per week while having an extra day off to stimulate the economy.
- 3-day x 10 hours (-2 weeks vacation): An awesome environment where workers feel like their job is a fun place to hang out and excited to put in those extra couple hours, and have two additional days to be consumers, doubling their free-days to take vacation, despite having less of it, spend money, and inject wealth into society.

## Even more benefits

There are even more benefits to eliminating work days in favor of longer and more efficient days.
- Less driving (a reduction in carbon emissions) for those who go into the office, this also means less of their time driving and even more time they get back to themselves.
- More opportunity for people to go on vacation and become refreshed (they don’t even need to use vacation days to get a 4-day vacation)
- More opportunity to inject money into the very jobs and companies that these people are working at, since it is people who spend money, and most of that money is spent when people aren’t working. (Henry Ford even talked about this as his motivation for the 5-day work-week)
