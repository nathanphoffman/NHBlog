Stars are Mindbogglingly Bright

Recently Cody’s Lab a science Youtuber posted a nice video on how distant the nearest stars are to Earth. For me it isn’t the distance that is surprising, it is thinking about how energetic stars must be to see from so far away. Before continuing you may want to watch this video where he shows just how far away some of the nearest stars to us are:

Walk out under an afternoon sky and it is pretty obvious that our Sun is intense, but have you thought to consider how intense?

Our Sun is 93 million miles away, yet from that distance it can burn your skin, light the entire planet and cause permanent eye damage if we look at it for prolonged periods of time.

93 million miles (150 million km) is just a number, so to put it into perspective, if you left Earth at 30 years old with your newborn child in your 4 door sedan at 60 miles per hour (97km/hr), each child had a child at 30, and each person died at 80, then by the time you would arrive at the sun:

  • You died of old age 96 years ago
  • Your child died of old age 66 years ago
  • Your grandchild died of old age 36 years ago
  • Your great grandchild died of old age 6 years ago
  • Your great great grandchild is 56, and the oldest living family member
  • Your great great great grandchild is 26 and will soon have your great great great great grandchild.

Now lets consider a common energetic source like a giant bonfire. A large bonfire might project enough intense heat to be felt from up to 100 feet away which would take a 60 mile per hour car just over 1 second to traverse, and even at a small distance wouldn’t come close to the energy we feel from the Sun here on Earth.

The largest nuclear blast was the Tsar Bomb at 50 megatons and even though it was an atomic weapon using both fission and fusion, heat could be felt up to about 430 miles (700km) away which would take our hypothetical car only 7 hours to traverse.

Now consider the sun, it would take us 146 years to get there yet so intense, burns your skin, lights our planet, gives life to all organisms and provides many times more power than 4 billion humans need once we have enough solar panels to absorb its light.

If all of this wasn’t enough to blow your mind, the other fact making this more astonishing is that light decreases by the square law because surface area (a sphere) is one power higher than a single dimension (diameter) so each time you double your distance you 1/4 the light’s power.
