11/27/2013 Archives: Comet ISON Now on SOHO!

Comet ISON began entering the field of SOHO’s LASCO C3 imager last night! It will continue to stay in the field for a few days and should appear on the “more zoomed-in” LASCO C2 imager in time for Thanksgiving. Scientists and amateur astronomers alike are hoping the comet can withstand the extreme temperatures and gravitational stress the comet will undergo as it comes within 724,000 miles of the Sun (about 50 times closer than Mercury). If the comet is able to withstand temperatures hot enough to melt iron, there is a good chance it will be a naked-eye object with a somewhat reasonable chance it could become bright enough to be considered a “Great Comet” It should be noted, however, that comets are notoriously unpredictable so ISON’s brightness (even if it does survive) could be wildly different than predicted.

SOHO, the Solar Dynamics Observatory, is a NASA space-telescope. It takes photos of the Sun multiple times every hour to provide a near-live view of the sun to scientists and the public.
