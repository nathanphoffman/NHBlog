3/14/2013 Archives: Happy Pi Day

To celebrate Pi day, I have listed a number of random astronomy facts associated with the number 314:

MWC 314 (an alternate designation for V1429 Aquilae) is a blue hypergiant star located in the constellation Aquila. MWC 314 is 25,000K in temperature and 1.2 million times more luminous than our sun.

On 3/14/1995, Norman Thagard became the first American astronaut to ride aboard a Russian launch vehicle.

Giovanni Schiaparelli, an Italian astronomer, who made contributions to the study of Mars, was born on 3/14/1835

314 Rosalia is a large Main belt asteroid about 57km in diameter.

On 3/14/1928, Frank Borman, the commander of Apollo 8, and one of the first three humans to orbit the Moon, was born.

4 Vesta, the second largest asteroid in the solar system, has a monstrous crater (bigger than most asteroids) which is 314 miles in diameter.

The lunar module pilot of Apollo 10, and the last man to set foot on the moon, Eugene Cernan was born on 3/14/1934.

Thrymr, a small moon of Saturn, has a semi-major axis (longest orbital radii) of 23,314,335km

Jupiter orbits the sun once every 11 years, 314 days.
