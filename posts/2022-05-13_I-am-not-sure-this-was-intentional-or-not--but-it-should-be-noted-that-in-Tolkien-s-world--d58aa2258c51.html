I am not sure this was intentional or not, but it should be noted that in Tolkien's world "Earendil" was an important half-elf who ended up in a ship flying across the sky with a Silmaril on his brow. It his him that Galadriel refers to the phial she gives Frodo:

"[..].the light of Eärendil's star, set amid the waters of my fountain. It will shine still brighter when night is about you. May it be a light to you in dark places, when all other lights go out."

Figured it was a fun fact to mention here.
