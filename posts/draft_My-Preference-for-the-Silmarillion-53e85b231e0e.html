My Preference for the Silmarillion

It was no secret that Tolkien’s true love was in the Silmarillion, specifically the Ainundale, years of the lamps, trees, and the first age. He plead with his publisher to publish it, threatened to leave them, and actually did just to find his new publisher let him out to dry leading to him coming back with his tail between his legs. Given the success of the Hobbit, Tolkien was only able to publish its intended sequel, Lord of the Rings.

He began writing the Silmarillion as far back as 1917 (20 years before the publication of the hobbit) with some of the first tales being Earendil, The Fall of Gondolin, and The Children of Hurin. It took on multiple conceptions from The Book of Lost Tales where the Silmarillion is told in frame-narrative, versions of several stories as poems, and many many many revisions of the Silmarillion in its high-biblical style that it was published in.

I have many issues with both the Hobbit and Lord of the Rings, for the Hobbit my issues are numerous and I would call it just an ok book of fantasy. For the Lord of the Rings my issues are less numerous but no more less serious and thus I would call it a good work of fantasy. But for me the Hobbit and the Lord of The Rings are weak echoes of the Silmarillion. Longer, more drawn out (especially LOTR), generally less serious yet also very serious which creates tonal issues. What I have always found compelling about these two works is much of the underlying world, lore, and especially creatures like the Balrog, Dragons, or what is Gandalf? And these are much more prominient in the Silmarillion and much more interesting.

The Lord of the Rings excels at some of its quotable lines, you can tell Tolkien spent huge amounts of time on specific important lines of dialog, important scenes, and beats, but he also spent time pounding away trying to fill gaps in material with seemingly less so thought like bumbling travel sequences and things to amuse himself and few others.

The Silmarillion is:

  • It was where Tolkien started writing the world of Arda
  • It is much higher fantasy than the hobbit and esp. LOTR
  • It is more adult, free from the mixed tones of the hobbit and the sometimes mixed tones of LOTR
  • It is not as meandering as either the hobbit or LOTR because it is a series of shorter stories.
  • It is dense, rather than fluffy, hundreds of years can literally be traversed in a few pages whereas in LOTR we go from Sam cooking something to Frodo setting up his bed that same night.
  • The silmarillion feels like a world, lore, campaign, bestiary, and adventure compendium all in one, almost like an RPG book moreso than LOTR which is just an adventure in a world.
  • I like that not all of the ancestries are tropy: elves have clearly different factions, dwarves are not all the same (including petty dwarves vs regular dwarves), even humans split into multiple categories like the numenoreans who live for much much longer and have tech and magic more like the elves. Dragons are wildly varied with Glaurong being a disgusting wingless dragon or worm that can cast spells and angelicon the black being as large as a mountain with a traditional dragon form.
  • There are direct influences from the gods, they all have personalities, and multiple interactions with the people of Arda, adding to the D&D feel and high fantasy nature of the world. Where as in LOTR it is at best suggested with Gandalfs return.
  • There is a spelljammer ship (or at least a literal star ship — Earendel’s ship vingolot set aloft) I mean comeon enough said lol.
  • Because it is a collection of short stories various types of tales are able to be told, there is a classic tragedy (Children of Hurin), a love story (Beren and Luthien), a mammoth battle (The War of Wrath), multiple downfalls of kingdoms (Ruin of Doriath, Fall of Gondolin), Genesis Tales (Ainundale), Betrayal & Greed (Feanor and the Silmarils), etc.
