6/5/2014 Archives: Cosmos Review

When I first saw the trailers for the Cosmos reboot series, A Spacetime Odyssey, I was very worried it would be a mindless cgi-packed documentary with none of the original’s intellectual value or awe-inspiring content. I was also deeply concerned about the use of animation, and feared it would take the place of actual photos and scientifically simulations.

Now that I have finished watching the series, I can say that my fears were unjustified. The new Cosmos series, hosted by Neil Degrasse Tyson, is a stunning and worthy successor to the original series. Like Carl Sagan, Neil delivers an enthusiastic presentation of the subject matter in a digestible fashion, and everything is written to a level of standard beyond other TV documentaries. As with Sagan’s Cosmos, the content is basic, but also intriguing and understandable by the whole family. It covers historical ground by use of animation, and contrary to my initial fears, does not use animation for anything other than historical demonstration.

The series also pays respect to Sagan and his original series. Neil tells several stories about his experiences with Sagan, including a personal visit with Sagan where he was given a signed book and Carl’s personal phone number. Some of the same locations shown during the original Cosmos, were used on the new cosmos. This was not due to a lack of creativity, but a new take on Carl’s views and a tribute to his legacy. The cosmic calendar is also redone in a great amount of detail, providing better clarity to the visual representation of cosmic history.

If I were to be extremely critical, I could find a few faults with the new series. I could mention that Neil’s performance seemed as if it had been rehearsed and memorized, a contrast to Carl’s genuine and non-scripted feel. I could also mention that some of the scenes (like Neil’s ship flying through the asteroid belt) were not scientifically accurate. However, these would be minor complaints.

Overall, Cosmos has delivered on what it set out to do. It has explained the cosmos with stunning visuals and scripts that personally connect with the audience. It has delivered an accurate scientific and historical balance of astronomical facts, and it has paid tribute to Carl Sagan and his original series, Cosmos.

The Cosmos is now on blu-ray. The quality is quite impressive, and extras are included on the disc. The extras include an entertaining Comic-Con visit by Neil, an interesting making of, and a touching tribute to Carl Sagan.

By Nathan P. Hoffman on March 24, 2017.
