# I’m Back!

Posted on Medium: 2025-11-09

![](/images/1-oiP5GtH1gq-svtlExmDXxw.png)

I have been away from my blog for sometime, a lot has happened in the last few years: I have lost around 100lbs. I have gone to the gym nearly every day curing my back pain, completely changed my diet going plant-based as part of my weight loss strategy, lowered my cholesterol 80-90 points, dropped my blood pressure 40 points, I have completely changed my outlook on life (more on that later), worked on a few projects of passion like a story I am working on and several TTRPG designs (tabletop role-playing games); I also worked on a few fun coding projects (more on those in future posts), but most relevant for this blog: I also began to question my career (spoiler: I am not questioning it any longer and expect more upcoming blog posts).

## How My Career Started

Back in the long long ago, when I was in my teenage years I was pretty much interested in 4 things: modding video games, LAN parties, creating websites, and space. I dreamed of someday programming for a company as my dad did. He used to print out his code which I saw as some kind of strange language that I desperately wanted to decode myself, and my dad’s friend would talk for long hours with my dad about technical jargon which to me was like magical elvish that would someday reveal itself to me.

As college fell through for me (a story too long for this post), I pursued community college while working full time. However, I was discouraged from being a programmer because of my dad’s experience with outsourcing (where he saw many of the people he was friends with have to walk out the doors - never to return). To be safe, I declared math as my major but was picked up by a REIT company as a desktop support technician before I could finish out an associates.

That desktop support position quickly became a programming position when a Jr. Dev spot opened and what began were my happiest years as a programmer. It was a dream of mine to be a coder, even today anytime I doubt it, all I need to do is sit down with some Typescript, Python, C#, or Rust: it just makes me smile.

## My Career Doubts

So why the doubt over my career then? While it has been something I have struggled with since COVID, I have finally come around to the answer. The best way I can summarize it is with a series of equations:

```
(poor_physical + poor_mental) = negative_outlook
(remote_work + little_collaboration) = dissatisfaction
(negative_outlook + dissatisfaction) + coding = negative_code_opinion
(tech_layoffs + ai_tools) = coding_market_poor

Finally:
negative_code_opinion + coding_market_poor = why am I a programmer?
```

## Breakdown

Many of these questions in my mind started over COVID. Being a single person with no kids in my early 30s trapped in an apartment alone while my siblings were having their first children made me look inward in a way I never had, and looking in the mirror at my huge belly, empty bed, and remote work, made my home look more like a chain and shackle than the freeing collaborative environment I was used to for 10 years. Worse, this drove heavier eating and while I had managed to come down to about 290 (which was light for me at the time) I rebounded to 315+ over the start of COVID, and this had yet another effect which fed my negativity.

One of the biggest negative sources for me was remote work. I know many friends who love it, and I am fully in favor of them having that freedom, but for me I need to be in person with people to enjoy what I do. This also relates to collaboration, as without in-person enjoyment I have found collaboration is muted mostly because of the annoyance of online tools blocking social discourse and the difficulty of reaching others.

Finally, I have had some friends looking for jobs (albeit not many looking for programming jobs) and they have found it hard to get positions in the industry, which has soured my prospects and my hope that I might find something in person I could really enjoy. Blend this with the shadow of AI, which while I don’t mind needs to be weighed pragmatically if it is going to limit job opportunities.

## **Conclusion**

The human mind is difficult to understand, but I think what happened to programming for me over this time was a bit like a Pavlovian response but in reverse. Rather than a dog drooling over things associated with food, I was repulsed by things associated with programming, but like the dog enjoying the food, I still enjoyed the programming if I could get through those negative feelings. Nothing has proven this more than a yo-yo phenomenon I have experienced over the last year or two of off-and-on again programming: I take long lulls when I abandon my personal projects because it was hard to penetrate those negative feelings and restart the engine.

The good news is the negative impulse is vanishing, and the more I push through, the more I retrain myself, and the easier it is to get myself into my coding. This is the main reason for this post as I am going to be posting more here in the future as I continue to code more and more for fun.

## A Word of Advice (If you find yourself where I did)

Something that has helped me get through all of this is Wu-Wei. In very loose translation this means “To Go with the Flow” but I think Alan Watts describes this better as being in a sailboat and guiding your sails with purpose but without forcing them. So if you see an island you really want to visit but the winds keep blowing you away, consider taking the long way around, and if the long way still doesn’t get you to that island, maybe it wasn’t the place for you to go all along. And who knows during your journey you might discover new places of happiness and new things about yourself. Life isn’t about forcing yourself into something you envision but listening to the changing universe and allowing it to shape you and enjoying the shaping in the way wind or water shapes stone into something much more beautiful than its coarse state.

Right now my ship has found itself back on the shore of programming, how long it will be here I do not know, maybe it will take off again and I will finally give up on it altogether, or perhaps I will continue to venture inland for many years to come, discovering the secrets of this place that sparked my child-imagination more than anything so many years ago.
