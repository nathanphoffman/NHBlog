# Some thoughts of wisdom

Posted on Medium: 2026-05-26

![](/images/1-zDxA0pM5iEz3Msa-uDiySA.png)

*Photo of Pluto from New Horizon’s flyby. Credit: NASA*

As a quick break from coding, I decided to look into a bit more Zen Buddhism and Taoism. I have long been a fan of Thich Nhat Hanh, Alan Watts, and Carl Sagan and the way they broke the universe down philosophically, spiritually and scientifically. I relate very much to their ideas as I connect most with their universal contentedness in their teachings — something that has been most apparent through my telescope through the coldest winter nights and dampest spring mornings.

I worked a little with some existing quotations, some suggestions from AI, a few ideas myself, and some editing and arrived at 10 words of wisdom, enjoy! My next post should be coding related, I have been going pretty hard on it.
1. You did not arrive into the universe, you were always here — the universe is you.
2. Spirituality requires no supernatural scaffolding — reality is plenty super and natural.
3. Stop fighting the current, let it carry you — a good sailor doesn’t fight the wind, they listen to it.
4. Live with less — if you think you need more, you already have too much.
5. Meditation is just sitting alone with yourself. Don’t complicate the sitting.
6. If you don’t live for today then you already died yesterday.
7. Grudges are invisible to the atom, molecule, cell, planet, star, and galaxy — what about you?
8. The universe has survived for 14 billion years, whatever happens Tuesday is not going to break it.
9. Clouds don’t grieve when changing shape, nor when they change to rain — why do you?
10. Before enlightenment go to the gym — after enlightenment go to the gym, but it still costs 20 dollars per month.
