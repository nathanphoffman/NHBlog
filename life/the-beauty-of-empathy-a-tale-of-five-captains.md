# The Beauty of Empathy & A Tale of Five Captains

Posted on Medium: 2022-11-05

![](/images/0-cvZUynqhAyWd9aYo.jpg)

Empathy and “acknowledging feelings” is more important than you know.

As humans, we often complain; it feels like it is in our blood. As I walk along at parks I often hear people venting about their lives or the people they know. In many workplaces people will vent about their coworkers or managers or they will speak out about their unhappiness on a project or challenge they are involved in. Even when no one is present to hear us, we nevertheless feel a compulsion to express ourselves, cursing in the room, and on occasion physically relieving the frustration: a foot tap, a sigh, or a slam of our desk.

Formality and “decency” has taught us that we shouldn’t complain, and yet holding these feelings in can be destructive, and as much as we would all like ourselves to have the compassion of Buddhist monks or the indifference to adversityof robots, the reality is the vast majority of us are not like that. We cannot sit in burning flames and not react to it. We feel, we suffer, we are human.

Empathy, however, is the antidote. Acknowledging each other’s feelings, comforting each other, and agreeing with how we feel is the evolutionary mechanism to relieve this pain. **Why? Because our brain *believes* that empathy will *eventually* solve problems.** Because empathy is equivalent to gaining the support of people around you. And evolution understands well that people solve problems much better collectively than alone.

## **The Five Captains**

**Crewman:** “Captain. We are sinking! We are sinking, I need help!”

**The Best Captain:** We are going down! *rings bell* Close all hatches! Deploy lifeboats! Let us get this under control! *The problem is resolved to the best of their ability.*

**The Great Captain**: We are sinking?Oh man I thought I noticed we might be going down, I will do my best although I can’t promise I can successfully stop it. *Affirms the persons feelings (empathy), agrees with the issue, and doesn’t even have to commit to fully resolving the problem.*

**The Good Captain:** We are sinking? Oh man if you saw us sinking, that is really bad. I will see what I can do, although I can’t promise I can successfully stop it. *Affirms the persons feelings (empathy) without the need to agree that the ship is sinking, and doesn’t even have to commit to fully resolving the problem.*

**The Bad Captain:** We are sinking? Stop joking, that doesn’t happen, even a god can’t sink this ship. Grow up. *Denies the person’s feelings but in such a blatant way that the crewman is likely to replace their sadness with anger. The apparent recklessness of their captain is obvious that this will likely to lead to a mutiny. The fact that this behavior is easy to understand is what makes this behavior less damaging to the crewman but is still quite Bad.*

**The Terrible Captain:** We are sinking? Are you sure you saw us sinking? Let us talk about all the other reasons you might feel this way and why it might have looked like we are sinking. Let us also bullet point 10 solutions to this “sinking problem” without actually doing anything significant. *Denies the persons feelings are as serious as they are, attempts to manipulate their feelings while not actually fixing the problem and pretends like the problem can still be fixed by disguising negligible or ineffective fixes as effective ones. If done extremely well, the captain might succeed in convincing the crewman that the ship is not sinking, or the sinking problem is not as bad as the thought. This may avoid a mutiny, but at a terrible cost. The ship will likely sink and the crewmen will begin to question their sanity. They will be less likely to raise issues in the future and are likely to fall into a self-destructive depression.*

## So does this mean we should all just complain?

No, but we shouldn’t bottle-up our feelings either; in general, file complaints in a private space and with someone who is:
- A person in an authority position who is responsible for the issue at hand: a manager at work, a lawyer/judge in legal case, a parent if you have an issue with their child, etc. In general these parties are less likely to cause offense since they are acknowledged as official channels and they are more likely to be able to be the “Best Captains” who can actually resolve the problem.
- A person who doesn’t know any parties involved. This might be a psychologist for those with money, or a very distant connection like an online friend who you only know through the internet and doesn’t have anything invested in the situation.
- A very close friend or significant other to limit the scope of your negative feelings and prevent it from becoming gossip.

What if I don’t have these options available and I am really upset? Honestly, then use your best judgement. You must weigh if the harm of some of the dark sides of empathy (gossip, tribalism, etc.) is greater than the harm the problem is causing you.

## Conclusion

This compulsion of seeking out humans to vent and open up our feelings is baked into us. In the prehistoric past, humans who were alone and unable to solve problems died. This drive to connect with other humans was essential when we were fighting for our lives on the Serengeti, and while the actual real-world costs of Empathy are no longer as significant, our brains don’t know that. So the next time someone complains to you, validate their feelings even if you don’t approve of them, and give them a shoulder to cry on. Thanks to our silly brains, this will make that person feel like you resolved their problem.

## A Note about Professionalism & Compassion

It may be said that it is not always possible to be an empathetic person (a Good/Great captain) if you are someone seeking to avoid an unprofessional attitude. After-all, how can you not defend someone like the owner or executive of a company being attacked by your employee? How can you not defend your beloved family member being attacked by a person you don’t know well? Its simple: by empathizing with them you are actually making the person feel as if their issue is resolved, and this will make them more likely to listen to your advice as they will gain respect for you. Paradoxically, you might even be able to cash in on that respect and convince them to talk with the very person they hate and form a better connection with them in the future.

Being silent on the behalf of the attacked party is not an attack you yourself are making, and it should be noted that even in the darkest of situations where you feel you need to debate the person, encouraging people and empathizing with them is still the better approach.
