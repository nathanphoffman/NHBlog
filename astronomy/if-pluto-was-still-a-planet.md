# If Pluto was still a Planet

Posted on Wordpress: 2014-03-24

Posted on Medium: 2017-04-14

![](/images/1-SuD1Vj_7Mml7jbObekAdUA.jpeg)

*Best image we currently have of Pluto (Credit: Hubble/NASA)*

It has been more than 7 years since Pluto was demoted from planetary status, but the intense negative-reaction from the public seems to have never died down. I was recently searching for information on Pluto and ran across several user comments about how the IAU’s decision to demote Pluto was blind and unjust. This got me to thinking, what would the Solar System be like if Pluto were still considered a planet? **The Current Planetary Model with Pluto as a Planet**
- Pluto would be one of 5 new planets (there would be 13 planets as of today, 2014)
- There would be at least 6 more planets added within the next few years, giving us 19 planets (there are 6 known objects which are pending IAU promotion to dwarf planet status)
- In the next few centuries there could be more than 100 known planets.
- Pluto would not be the most massive of the newly added planets (Eris would)
- Pluto would no longer be the furthest object in the solar system, three planets would be further (upsetting folks who romanticized its lone distant spot in the solar system)
- In a few more years, 8 planets would be classified as further (with one of those 8, Sedna, averaging about 10 times further from the Sun)
- There would be 7 moons larger than the 5 newly added planets (including Pluto)
- In a few years, our moon would be larger than more than 1/2 the planets in the solar system (including Pluto)
- There would be 16 moons larger than at least one of the newly added planets.
- Only 2/3 of the continental US would be needed to wrap the entire surface of the smallest newly added planet.
- Ganymede, the largest moon, would have twice the surface area as all 5 new planets combined!
- At least two asteroids in the asteroid belt would be planets. (Vesta and Ceres)
