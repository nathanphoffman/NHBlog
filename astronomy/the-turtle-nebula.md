# The Turtle Nebula

Posted on Wordpress: 2014-06-07

Posted on Medium: 2017-03-31

![](/images/1-gmsWXLi0hNr-8CZW5C39og.jpeg)

*The Turtle Nebula (Central Region) Credit: NASA*

I was doing some research on objects in Stellarium (astronomy software) and found little online about the Turtle Nebula, which made me want to document what I found:

The Turtle Nebula (NGC 6210) is a planetary nebula around 5 or 6 thousand light years away in Hercules. It is a gaseous cloud which has been expelled from a dying star.

Structurally it has several jets of gas extending from the main cloud and an active central-region. Its interior is approximately 14 x 20 arc-seconds and its exterior extends out to more than 30 arc-seconds. This means that the exterior region appears from Earth to be 60 times smaller than our Moon, difficult to magnify though traditional amateur equipment. Despite its small apparent size, it is actually about 0.4 lightyears across. If it replaced our sun, it, it would reach about 10% of the way to our closest stellar neighbor Proxima Centauri.

For a planetary nebula, it is fairly bright. It has a magnitude of 9.30 and a high surface brightness of 15.1Mag/arc-sec². This means it looks more like a star than a nebulous object when observed through small aperture, low magnification (fast f-ratio), telescopes. The nebula has been reported to be sky-blue in color when observed through amateur photos/equipment.
