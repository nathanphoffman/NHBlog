# 3/14/2013 Archives: Happy Pi Day

![](/images/1-RQpqhXpNagWDN6NQ1nyVDA.jpeg)

To celebrate [Pi day](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pi_Day), I have listed a number of random astronomy facts associated with the number 314:

MWC **314** (an alternate designation for [V1429 Aquilae](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/V1429_Aquilae)) is a blue hypergiant star located in the constellation Aquila. MWC 314 is 25,000K in temperature and 1.2 million times more luminous than our sun.

On **3/14/**1995, [Norman Thagard](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norman_Thagard) became the first American astronaut to ride aboard a Russian launch vehicle.

[Giovanni Schiaparelli](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giovanni_Schiaparelli), an Italian astronomer, who made contributions to the study of Mars, was born on **3/14**/1835

[**314** Rosalia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/314_Rosalia) is a large Main belt asteroid about 57km in diameter.

On **3/14**/1928, [Frank Borman](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Borman), the commander of Apollo 8, and one of the first three humans to orbit the Moon, was born.

[4 Vesta](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/4_Vesta), the second largest asteroid in the solar system, has a monstrous crater (bigger than most asteroids) which is **314** miles in diameter.

The lunar module pilot of Apollo 10, and the last man to set foot on the moon, [Eugene Cernan](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_Cernan) was born on **3/14**/1934.

[Thrymr](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thrymr_%28moon%29), a small moon of Saturn, has a semi-major axis (longest orbital radii) of 23,**314**,335km

[Jupiter](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jupiter) orbits the sun once every 11 years, **314** days.
