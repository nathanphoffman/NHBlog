# TimeScapes: A Desktop Wallpaper Movie

Posted on Wordpress: 2012-06-12

Posted on Medium: 2017-08-11

I was watching a S[pacevidcast](http://www.spacevidcast.com/) and saw an advertisement for TimeScapes, an incredible 4096 x 2304 adventure through stunning footage taken by Tom Lowe, the [Astronomy Photographer of 2010](http://www.rmg.co.uk/visit/exhibitions/astronomy-photographer-of-the-year/winners-2010/earth-and-space/category-winners-earth-and-space) for Earth and Space. I just bought the 1080p version because I am apparently not awesome enough to need 4K resolution. The movie focuses quite a bit on the rising Milky Way as well as transitions from night to day. There are also other bits mixed in which I will not spoil for you, but the bottom line: most of it is beyond awesome.

That being said, I feel the price is a bit high for what is essentially a photography demo movie. I might be able to get past the ~$15-$20 digital 1080p price it was not for a lack of variety, as some of what you see is shown multiple times throughout the film. The film is also less than 40 minutes if you take all the intro and outro-credits out, which is made even shorter since the photographer mixes in random scenes of people dancing. Still, it is certainly worth the price if you are a fan of photography or astronomy, and I certainly don’t mind the price if it benefits individuals with an interest in astronomy. With that I will leave you a snapshot of part of the beginning of the movie and the subject which won Tom the Astronomy Photographer of 2010 title. Update: After watching the Making Of, I have a much greater appreciation for the the time these guys spent on this, and after a second watch, I am certain this is worth the $15.
