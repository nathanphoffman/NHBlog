# Apollo 17

Posted on Wordpress: 2012-06-10

Posted on Medium: 2017-08-18

![](/images/1-ndkS3ak5Ta3wmugnLAmtjQ.jpeg)

*Schmitt*

With the exception of Apollo 11 and the disastrous Apollo 1 and Apollo 13 missions, very few people know what happened during the rest of the Apollo program. By the time of Apollo 17, most of the media had lost interest in Apollo, but little would they know that these astronauts would be the last humans on the moon for a very very long time.

The Apollo 17 mission set many records: from length of extravehicular activities, to the amount of moon rock samples they returned. Among these samples was a [Troctolite rock](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troctolite_76535), found by Harrison Schmitt, which has been described as the “most useful lunar sample returned from the moon.” I will leave you with a photo of Schmitt next to a Lunar Boulder against the overwhelming darkness of space.
