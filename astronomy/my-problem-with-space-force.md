# My Problem with Space Force

Posted on Medium: 2022-04-28

![](/images/0-qwxeXSVpngvNlXp7.jpg)

*The Apollo-Soyuz program, a collaboration between The United States and The Soviet Union*

*I want to emphasize before I begin this post that I am not posting this because of any political position; I also am not making any kind of commentary on the war going on right now.*

To help understand why I have problems with Space Force, we need to go back in time to 1969, when Apollo 11 was getting ready to come home. Before leaving, the astronauts dropped off:
- A mission patch in memory of the astronauts who died in the Apollo 1 fire
- Two memorial medals of the Soviet cosmonauts Vladimir Komarov and Yuri Gagarin who had lost their lives.
- A memorial bag containing a gold olive branch (a symbol of peace)
- A silicon statement containing goodwill messages from several US presidents and messages from the leaders of 73 other countries.

![](/images/0-TjN0QAUPQp_wL-rS.png)

The Apollo 11 crew was also the only Apollo mission not to show their names on their patches. When asked about it, Michael Collins stated “We wanted to keep our three names off it because we wanted it to be representative of everyone who worked toward a lunar landing.” An olive branch was even added to the Eagle’s claws to represent a peaceful expedition, based on a suggestion from Tom Wilson, a computer expert at one of the simulators.

NASA, and those involved, made every effort to go to The Moon in peace, not out of some kind of military achievement, but as a NASA achievement; a US achievement, and perhaps more importantly: a human achievement.

This mission of peaceful exploration was not isolated to Apollo 11, and would be repeated by NASA time and again, in fact one of the greatest achievements of NASA (in my opinion) was setting aside their differences with the Soviet Union on the Apollo-Soyuz program where two large modules were docked in Earth-orbit to create a mini-space station which would lay the groundwork for the International Space Station, a collaboration of many of the world’s countries.

Yet 30 years after the Apollo-Soyuz success, the cracks started to show when the United States refused to let China join the space station. *China has since started their own space station (Tiangong).* The United States was willing to collaborate with The Soviet Union, our sworn enemy in a unique moment of peaceful cooperation during the Cold War in 1981, yet in 2010 they denied a country that we are not sworn enemies with into that same international collaboration.

The cracks became even wider with the talk of a “Space Force,” a military organization, the closed fist of NASA’s other hand. Worse, they used symbolism oddly similar to that of the fictional Star Trek Federation badges, a show which famously attempted to show the better side of humanity; a symbol which the Chinese space agency has already repurposed for themselves.

![](/images/1-RylId1oDWJGMBBvryks5rg.png)

*United States Space Force (Left), Star Trek (Middle), China National Space Administration (Right)*

To me, Space Force seems like a direct response to other countries entering space more and more. The United States is intentionally trying to get ahead with an aggressive posture, metaphorically dropping the olive branch and clutching the arrow in its hand.

When I think about what is needed for the future of spaceflight, I don’t think it is more military power, I think what is needed is hope for the future, which involves talking rather than fighting.

> When you are talking, you are not fighting, you are talking. It is when the talking stops, that fighting begins. — Daryl Davis

Talking brought down the Berlin wall, talking avoided Nuclear annihilation in the Kennedy administration, talking helped bring two countries together in joint-space collaborations in the 1980s and talking is what we need more of in new frontiers.

NASA is a civilian arm, the hand of us, the people. Space Force is a military arm, the hand of the military & the president. I understand the purpose of a military here on Earth, but a military in space only makes a statement that we want to bring warfare into the future so that our children and their children can continue to fight one another.

Please, as we are setting out into space and bumping into each other in new territories; let us shake the hand of our adversaries with the warm hands of civil servants, not with the closed fists of war.
