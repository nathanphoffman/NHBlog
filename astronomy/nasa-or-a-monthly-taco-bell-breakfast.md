# NASA or a Monthly Taco Bell Breakfast

Posted on Wordpress: 2014-03-27

Posted on Medium: 2017-04-07

![](/images/1-AYfIE6MxQxDEhtYX4mDeKA.jpeg)

Many Americans believe that NASA costs a lot more than it actually does. In truth, NASA’s budget is less than 1/2 of 1 percent, or about 0.48% of the Federal Budget. (16.6 Billion dollars as of 2014). It is still a lot of money, but divided by 314 million Americans, this represents $4.41/month/person. To convert that into numbers hungry Americans understand, that is about 2 Waffle Tacos with some room for sales tax. If we do not make children and elderly pay, then those of working ages are now paying $5.51/mo! That is a waffle taco AND a steak and egg burrito with perhaps enough leftover for my favorite, a hashbrown :). Getting pricey, I know. Now if we take into account that only 67% of that age group are actually working, then it costs a whopping $8.22/month per working adult. That would be nearly everything you see below! Save a few Cinnabon delights.

So next time you lazily slide that plastic card, mumble thanks, and bite into that waffle taco, just remember to buy an extra couple of tacos and save a seat for inspiration, education, satellites, spaceflight, GPS, weather monitoring systems, solar monitoring systems, documentaries (Aliens!), exoplanet discoveries, solar system exploration, telescopic observations, and beautiful Hubble wallpapers.
